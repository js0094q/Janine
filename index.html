About Janine

She/Her/Hers

For nearly 20 years, I have provided treatment across multiple settings including private practice, community mental health centers, hospitals, schools, yoga studios and in-home treatment.

I obtained my B.A. in Psychology from Rutgers University, followed by graduate degrees in both Psychological Studies (M.A.) & Marriage and Family Therapy (Ed.S.) from Seton Hall University. I am a Licensed Professional Counselor in the State of New Jersey. I am a certified adult and youth yoga instructor. This paired with my knowledge of neuropsychological principles and the impact of stress, are the ideal combination for promoting wellness.

As a private practice owner, I provide individual, couple and family treatment, as well as yoga based wellness programming and private mindfulness coaching.

My therapeutic services are customized to meet the specific needs of each client/family. I am engaged throughout the entire therapeutic process and guarantee measurable results.

"Happy Spouse, Happy House"

Building relationships that cultivate joy, connection and intimacy - one breath, one step, one day at a time.

We often focus on curating the "perfect" wedding day, invitation, living space or social media post with/about our partner. Yet, rarely do we consider the skills we need to create a life together that is grounded in connection, communication and intention. My goal is to provide a space for you to collaboratively build the foundation for a lasting partnership.

Begin your journey with relational alignment so you can flourish individually and together.

Who can benefit?

This program is for any partnership desiring to enhance their connection. Whether you are looking for a non-denominational pre-marital program or simply to expand your connection, this program is for you!

The Skills

The first 6 sessions focus on skill building - expanding introspection, understanding attachment, engaging in healthy communication and enhancing connection. The result is more rewarding conversations, greater intimacy and the ability to effectively navigate inevitable conflicts.

The Lens

Sessions 7-12 focus on understanding the lens through which each partner sees the world, as well as exploring what has shaped each partner's view of relationships. This expanded view allows for fewer misperceptions and miscommunications which can lead to disagreements and disconnection.
